Release date: 2026-04-01

New

  • A new onboarding flow helps you get to your first working setup faster. First-run guidance is more structured and does a better job walking you through permissions, setup, and template creation.
  • Build custom prompts and templates in chat. You can now create and refine naming setups through a more conversational chat-first flow instead of configuring everything manually upfront.
  • LM Studio models can be loaded in-app. Browse, load, and manage LM Studio models without leaving NameQuick.
  • File dates are available as template fallback values. Use created, modified, or accessed dates when a document is missing stronger metadata.

Improvements

  • Presets and Rules have a redesigned editing experience. The updated split-view layout, inspector, and editor workspace make it easier to browse presets, edit rules, and refine naming behavior.
  • Creating templates in chat feels more guided and reliable. Activation, onboarding, and follow-up guidance now lead you more cleanly from first prompt to saved template.
  • AI model lists are now more up to date across providers. OpenAI, Gemini, and Claude now show newer supported models, and older saved selections are moved forward automatically when needed.
  • Template editing is more resilient when fields, patterns, and rules change together. Pattern updates, cleanup rules, placeholders, and reorder operations now stay in sync more reliably.
  • Unexpected AI output is handled more gracefully. NameQuick recovers more often, preserves valid filename output more accurately, and explains failures more clearly when a response cannot be used.
  • The update experience is easier to review in-app. Release notes and update details display more reliably in the banner and updater flow.
  • Local model and provider flows are more predictable. Selected-model behavior, provider routing, and local-model release handoffs now behave more consistently.

Bug Fixes

  • Fixed a licensing regression that could show the wrong trial or credit state after relaunch. Customer portal links and licensing details now stay in sync more reliably.
  • Fixed false filename parse failures for valid bracketed or brace-prefixed names. Outputs like [Draft], [], or {Draft:Q1} are no longer misclassified as broken JSON.
  • Fixed onboarding and coachmark handoff bugs that could leave guidance stuck or out of sync. Presets, chat, and setup transitions now complete more reliably.
  • Fixed stale navigation writes during activation flows. Route handoffs no longer complete on the wrong surface when an older activation step fires late.
  • Fixed several edge cases in template patching and rule editing. Remove, reorder, duplicate-name, and preserve-mode operations now behave more consistently.
  • Fixed process and timeout cleanup issues when external tools spawn child processes. Processing is more reliable and less likely to leave stuck or interrupted work behind.
  • Fixed invalid-output and metadata fallback issues across providers. Valid AI responses are less likely to be rejected, and retry behavior is more consistent when a provider fails mid-run.
